### ECS-specific use cases

**Endocannabinoid signaling.** Anandamide (AEA), 2-arachidonoylglycerol (2-AG), the synthesis enzymes (NAPE-PLD, DAGL), the degradation enzymes (FAAH, MAGL). Each compound and enzyme with its function, location, and the conditions that upregulate or downregulate its activity.

```
Topic: "Endocannabinoid degradation: FAAH and MAGL roles, inhibitor pharmacology, clinical implications"
→ Returns cards covering enzyme substrates, the consequences of inhibiting each enzyme, current clinical trial activity, and the rationale for selective vs dual inhibition strategies
```

**CB1 and CB2 receptor distribution.** CB1 in central nervous system, CB2 in immune system and peripheral tissue. Each receptor with its distribution map, primary signaling pathway (Gi/o coupling), downstream effects, and the populations where receptor density variation matters.

**Cannabinoid taxonomy.** Phytocannabinoids (THC, CBD, CBG, CBN, CBC), endocannabinoids (AEA, 2-AG), synthetic cannabinoids. Each class with its receptor affinity, pharmacological profile, and major distinctions.

**Terpenes and the entourage effect.** Major terpenes (myrcene, limonene, linalool, beta-caryophyllene, alpha-pinene) with their pharmacology and the evidence (or lack of evidence) for synergistic effects with cannabinoids.

**ECS in fitness and recovery.** Exercise-induced endocannabinoid release ("runner's high" mechanism), ECS modulation of pain perception, ECS roles in sleep-wake cycle regulation, ECS in inflammation resolution.

**ECS and the BVC stance.** How honest engagement with cannabinoid use fits the broader Better Vice Club framework. When does the ECS literature support cannabinoid as intervention? When does it support cannabinoid as the vice itself? Drilling the nuances that distinguish skilled practice from one-size-fits-all advocacy.

### AI answer grading handles cannabinoid pharmacology edge cases

ECS-specific answers have edge cases that frustrate naive grading:

- **Compound name variations.** "AEA" and "anandamide" treated equivalently. "2-AG" and "2-arachidonoylglycerol" both accepted.
- **Receptor terminology.** "CB1 receptor" and "cannabinoid receptor type 1" graded equivalently.
- **Common name vs IUPAC.** "THC" and "delta-9-tetrahydrocannabinol" both accepted for the major psychoactive cannabinoid.
- **Mechanism phrasings.** "FAAH degrades anandamide" and "fatty acid amide hydrolase hydrolyzes AEA" graded with similarity awareness.

### Versus Mode for cohort drilling

ECS specialization students often hold other practitioner credentials (CPT, CES, CNC) and study together. Versus Mode formalizes the competitive review: generate cards from this week's lesson, share a challenge code, see the leaderboard sort on accuracy plus speed plus confidence calibration.

The confidence-calibration component matters in cannabinoid practice more than most domains. The cannabis literature has gaps, contradictions, and active research fronts. A practitioner who confidently misstates a finding can propagate bad guidance to clients in ways that harm their experience or their results. Drilling with confidence ratings builds the habit of "this is established, this is provisional, this we don't know yet."
