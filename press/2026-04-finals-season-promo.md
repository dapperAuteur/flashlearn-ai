### What changed today

- **Free plan:** 3 AI-generated sets per 30 days on a normal cycle. **20 per 30 days through May 31.**
- **Monthly Pro:** 5 AI-generated sets per 30 days on a normal cycle. **20 per 30 days through May 31.**
- **Annual Pro and Lifetime Learner:** 10 AI-generated sets per 30 days on a normal cycle. **20 per 30 days through May 31.**
- **CSV imports:** unlimited on every plan, every cycle, finals or not.
- **New optional prompt field** on PDF, YouTube, audio, image, and text generations. Up to 500 characters. Sanitized server-side. Each generation still counts as one set whether or not a prompt is attached.

### Why the prompt field matters during finals

A pre-med student studying for an organic chemistry final can upload the same chapter PDF twice with different prompts. The first prompt asks for nomenclature drills. The second asks for reaction mechanism flashcards. Two generations, two focused decks, one source document. Before today, the same upload would produce one generic deck and the student would have to rebuild manually for each topic.

The same flow works across every input type:

- **PDF + prompt** for chapter readings with a target depth.
- **YouTube + prompt** for lecture recordings, isolating one topic the professor covered.
- **Audio + prompt** for recorded study sessions, asking for first-pass definition cards versus second-pass synthesis cards.
- **Image + prompt** for whiteboard photos, asking the model to focus on the equations and ignore handwritten margin notes.

### How the cap math works

All five AI inputs (text, PDF, YouTube, audio, image) count against one shared per-30-day counter. CSV does not count. A generation with a prompt counts the same as a generation without one. The promo replaces the per-tier ceiling for the duration of the window; it does not stack.

A student on Monthly Pro who generates 12 PDF sets, 4 audio sets, and 2 YouTube sets between now and May 31 has used 18 of their 20 promo allotment. They keep going until 20. After May 31 the cap reverts to 5; the same student starts a fresh rolling 30-day window per the standard cycle reset rules.

CSV imports during the window do not consume promo credits. A class study group can build 50 shared decks via CSV without spending an AI generation.
