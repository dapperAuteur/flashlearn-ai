### What BVC actually teaches

The premise is simple. Most people pretend their vices don't cost them anything, or quit cold turkey and relapse. BVC teaches a third path. Three stances anchor the curriculum:

1. **Every vice has a real cost and a real reason.** Naming both is the starting point.
2. **Abstinence is a tool, not a virtue. So is moderation. So is elimination.** Pick the tool that fits the vice.
3. **Interventions that survive contact with ordinary life are the only ones worth running.** Design for WinsDay, not New Year's Day.

### Class structure

The flagship Better Vice Club core walks through the vice audit, intervention design, and post-intervention adjustment. Companion curricula extend the framework into specific domains:

- **Foundations of Fitness.** First-principles fitness education grounded in BAM's NASM CPT, CES, and CNC credentials. The training, nutrition, and recovery substrate that makes BVC's interventions stick.
- **Intervention Design.** How to design behavioral and physical interventions that hold up. Draws on BAM's consulting work across developer relations, corrective exercise, and corporate training.
- **Endocannabinoid System.** ECS role in fitness, recovery, sleep, and vice regulation. Sourced from BAM's published infographic and the research backing it. Has its own dedicated [press release](2026-04-press-release-endocannabinoid-system.md).
- **FDAC** (Fitness Data Analytics Course). Data literacy for fitness practitioners. The applied intersection of fitness, diet, and daily conditioning. Has its own [press release](2026-04-press-release-fitness-data-analytics.md).
