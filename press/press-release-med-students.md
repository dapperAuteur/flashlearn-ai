**[City, State] — March 2026** — FlashLearnAI.WitUS.Online today announced the availability of its Public API for medical education, giving med students, residency programs, and medical education platforms access to AI-generated flashcard content with built-in spaced repetition scheduling.

Medical students face a unique challenge: memorizing thousands of terms, drug interactions, anatomical structures, and clinical protocols across years of study. Flashcards with spaced repetition have been the go-to study method since the rise of Anki, but creating quality flashcards is time-consuming and building study tools from scratch is impractical.

The FlashLearnAI.WitUS.Online API solves both problems. Generate flashcards from any medical topic — pharmacology, pathology, anatomy, biochemistry — with a single API call. The platform handles AI generation, content validation, and SM-2 spaced repetition scheduling automatically.

### Why This Matters for Med Students

**The volume problem:** A typical med student needs to learn 10,000+ facts across preclinical and clinical years. Manual flashcard creation takes hours that should be spent studying.

**The retention problem:** Without spaced repetition, students forget 90% of what they study within a week. The SM-2 algorithm used by FlashLearnAI.WitUS.Online — the same algorithm behind Anki — schedules reviews at scientifically-optimal intervals to maximize long-term retention.

**The competition problem:** Study groups and residency programs want to test knowledge competitively. The Versus Mode API enables quiz challenges where students are scored on accuracy (40%), speed (25%), confidence calibration (20%), and consistency (15%) — producing a composite score from 0 to 1,000.

### Spaced Repetition Analytics

The API provides per-card analytics that med students can use to identify weak areas:
- **Easiness factor** — Cards below 2.0 indicate topics the student struggles with
- **Interval** — How many days until the next review (longer = better mastery)
- **Next review date** — Exact scheduling for each card
- **Confidence data** — Tracks when students are confidently wrong (dangerous in medicine)
