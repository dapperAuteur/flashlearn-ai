### What Wanderlearn does differently

Most online learning is video and slides. Students watch a screen. Wanderlearn puts students inside the place.

- **360° vantage points.** Each lesson scene is a real location captured in 360° photography. The student rotates the view to look around. Click anywhere to look at it.
- **Place-anchored prompts.** Lessons aren't generic. Each prompt is tied to what the student is looking at right now in the scene. ("You're in MUCHO's Olmec exhibit. Notice the figurine on the left wall. Why is it holding a cacao pod?")
- **Self-paced movement through scenes.** Walk from the entrance hall to the tasting room when you're ready. The course doesn't gate progression on a clock.
- **Built for high school cognitive load.** Lessons run 8-12 minutes per scene. Long enough to teach something. Short enough to fit between classes.

### Why high school

Wanderlearn is built specifically for ages 13-19, grades 9-12. The platform is designed around this audience's cognitive load, attention span, scaffolding needs, and curriculum alignment. K-8 students benefit from different formats (more guided, fewer scenes per lesson). College students benefit from longer-form lectures with deeper reading lists. Wanderlearn's sweet spot is high school: rich enough to be intellectually substantive, structured enough to fit a class period.

### Who Wanderlearn is for

- **High school students** preparing for AP exams, IB exams, or general college-prep history / culture / science classes.
- **High school teachers** who want field-trip-quality learning experiences without the field-trip budget. Wanderlearn courses are appropriate for in-class projection or assigned-as-homework use.
- **Homeschool families** wanting structured place-based learning that complements text-based curricula.
- **Curious high schoolers** who consume YouTube documentaries about places and want a more interactive format.

### Accessibility

- Every scene has alt text describing the visual content for screen readers.
- Audio segments have captions.

- Vestibular-sensitivity mode locks the camera to a still frame so transitions don't spin.
