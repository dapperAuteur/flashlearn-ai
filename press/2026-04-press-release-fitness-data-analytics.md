### FDAC-specific use cases

**Wearable-data interpretation.** Heart rate variability (HRV), resting heart rate, sleep stages, recovery scores, training strain. Each metric with what it actually measures, what it doesn't measure, common artifacts, and the populations where the metric is most reliable.

```
Topic: "Heart rate variability (HRV) interpretation: RMSSD vs LF/HF, what populations have what baselines"
→ Returns cards covering the most-used HRV metrics, the difference between time-domain and frequency-domain analysis, age and sex baseline ranges, and common confounders (caffeine, alcohol, illness)
```

**Training-load metrics.** Acute load, chronic load, acute:chronic workload ratio (ACWR), session RPE, internal vs external load. Each metric with its calculation, its predictive limits, and the controversies around its use for injury prediction.

**Recovery markers.** Sleep stage interpretation, morning HRV trends, perceived recovery scales (Hooper, TQR). Each marker with its evidence base and its application in autoregulated programming.

**Statistical literacy basics.** Effect size vs p-value, confidence intervals, mean vs median, the difference between statistical and practical significance. The minimum stats vocabulary a fitness practitioner needs to read a study without getting fooled.

**Data-collection pitfalls.** Selection bias, regression to the mean, the file-drawer problem, the difference between correlation and causation in nutrition / exercise research. Useful for evaluating any new fitness-industry claim.

**Data-driven programming concepts.** Autoregulation, RPE-based loading, velocity-based training, RIR (reps in reserve) terminology. Cards drill the vocabulary that underlies modern programming methods.

### AI answer grading handles fitness-data edge cases

FDAC-specific answers have edge cases the AI handles:

- **Metric abbreviation variations.** "HRV" and "heart rate variability" treated equivalently. "ACWR" and "acute:chronic workload ratio" both accepted.
- **Statistical phrasing.** "p < 0.05" and "statistically significant at the 5% level" graded with similarity awareness.
- **Effect size interpretation.** Cohen's d ranges accepted in word form ("small," "medium," "large") or numeric form (0.2, 0.5, 0.8).
- **Wearable-brand specifics.** "Garmin Body Battery" and "Whoop Recovery Score" recognized as different (and not interchangeable) recovery metrics.

### Versus Mode for FDAC cohorts

FDAC enrollment runs as cohorts. Versus Mode formalizes the competitive review most cohorts develop organically: generate cards from this week's lesson, share a challenge code with the cohort, see the leaderboard sort on accuracy plus speed plus confidence calibration.

The confidence-calibration component is the whole point of FDAC. A practitioner who confidently misreads a wearable export, or confidently quotes a study they've misunderstood, propagates bad decisions into client programming. Drilling with confidence ratings builds the habit of "I know what I know, I flag what I don't."
