### Use-case posts (Weeks 2-4)

**[LinkedIn] Post 2.A: Pre-med advisors and instructors**

Pre-med pre-finals scenario. You teach a section of biochem, you know the high-yield USMLE-Step-1 content overlap, and you have a vetted PDF chapter your students should be drilling. Two minutes:

1. Create a study group called "Biochem Section 4 Spring 2026."
2. Upload the chapter PDF with this prompt: "USMLE-Step-1 high-yield format, definitions on front, mechanism on back, skip first-aid one-liners students already know."
3. Share the 6-digit code with the section.

Every student in the group studies the same deck. You can replace it next week with a new one for the next chapter. The deck still counts as one AI generation against your monthly cap.

### Mid-month posts (Week 4)

**[LinkedIn] Post 4.A: Instructor roundup**

Two weeks into the FlashLearnAI study group launch, what we have learned from college instructors using it:

- Study groups work better when the deck is curated, not student-generated. Instructors who upload one well-prompted PDF and share it consistently see higher engagement than groups where every member uploads their own.
- The 3-email invite cap is a feature, not a friction. It forces instructors to identify their cohort leaders intentionally.
- Free plan covers a section of 30+ when only the instructor uploads. Pro plans matter for instructors uploading multiple decks per week.

If you teach at a college and have not set this up yet, finals week is the test. Two minutes to spin up. [link]
