**[LinkedIn] Post 2.A: Why steerable prompts beat generic flashcards**
Generic AI flashcards are flat. They cover a topic at one depth, in one register, for one imagined audience.

The reality: a med student studying pharmacology has different needs the night before USMLE Step 1 versus the night before a 2L doctrine quiz. Same student. Same hour of sleep. Different study mode.

We just shipped a 500-character instructions field on every file upload at FlashLearnAI. PDF + audio + image + YouTube + plain text all accept the same kind of steering: target audience, depth, format, what to include, what to skip.

A few prompts that have come in from beta users this week:

- "Undergraduate intro level. Definitions only. Skip examples."
- "Graduate clinical board prep. Include differential diagnoses. Skip everything I'd already know from undergrad biology."
- "Bar exam MBE format. 4-option MCQ on the back. Civil procedure only."
- "Front in Spanish, back in English with a usage example. Skip cognates."

Each prompt produces a different deck from the same source document. Same one-generation cost.

Through May 31, every plan gets 20 of those generations per 30 days. Cap reverts June 1.
